Is SAI the Right Fit for You?

Our lab culture is built on collaboration, rigorous scientific inquiry, and mutual respect. We are looking for driven individuals who are ready to tackle complex challenges at the intersection of artificial intelligence and healthcare.

YES! IF...

  • You possess an insatiable curiosity for bridging artificial intelligence and translational medicine.
  • You have a genuine dedication to continuous learning, particularly regarding new algorithms, architectures, and clinical concepts.
  • You are enthusiastic about the research process and look forward to analyzing the results of a complex model training run.
  • You have a collaborative spirit and genuinely celebrate the successes and academic breakthroughs of your lab mates.
  • You are deeply passionate about leveraging science and engineering to solve real-world problems.

NO! IF...

  • You struggle to engage respectfully with diverse perspectives or lack professional courtesy.
  • You are resistant to feedback, constructive critique, or learning from the expertise of others.
  • You prefer working in silos and are hesitant to share code, data, resources, or knowledge with your team.
  • You take yourself too seriously to appreciate a lighthearted environment or share a laugh at a good joke with your colleagues.
  • You find it difficult to adapt to setbacks or lack the resilience to pivot when an approach or pipeline fails.
  • You are more attached to your initial hypotheses than you are to what the empirical data and clinical realities actually demonstrate.

How to Apply

If you believe your interests and work style align with ours, we would love to hear from you. Please contact Dr. Meenalosini Vimal Cruz at mcruz@georgiasouthern.edu and Mr. Akshay Bhuvaneswari Ramakrishnan at ab66943@georgiasouthern.edu.

Important Note: We value authentic, personal communication. Please do not use generative AI tools (such as ChatGPT, etc.) to draft your inquiry email or supporting materials. Applications containing AI-generated text will not be reviewed and will not receive a response.

In your email, please include:

  1. A brief, originally authored explanation of why you are specifically interested in joining SAI.
  2. An overview of your long-term career goals.
  3. Your primary technical or research background (e.g., Deep Learning, Signal Processing, Clinical Data Analysis).
  4. A copy of your current CV or resume.

Prospective Graduate Students

We welcome prospective and current graduate students from relevant programs at Georgia Southern University (such as Computer Science, Information Technology, Engineering, or related fields).

If you are enrolled in or applying to one of these programs and are interested in our research, please reach out to set up a meeting.
